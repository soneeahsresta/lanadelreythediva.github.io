White dresses, Priscilla Presley-esque eyeliner and, of course, soft, feminine florals. These are all things that have made Lana Del Rey the face of the “coquette” aesthetic, the hyper-feminine girly-girl style that’s taken the internet by storm.

Lana Del Rey has made a significant impact on the music industry, often credited with pioneering a unique blend of cinematic, nostalgic, and melancholic pop music that has influenced a generation of artists and listeners alike.

Lana Del Rey's impact on music is profound across aesthetics, sound, and storytelling. Introducing a unique blend of vintage Hollywood glamour and modern indie pop, her visual and musical aesthetic evokes themes of Americana, romance, and tragic beauty, creating a dreamy and emotive atmosphere. Musically, Del Rey blends pop, rock, indie, and alternative genres with lush orchestration, dreamy synths, and haunting vocals, sparking a resurgence of interest in retro and cinematic styles. Her introspective lyrics delve into themes of love, heartbreak, fame, and existential longing, crafting emotive narratives that have inspired a new era of lyrically-focused songwriting, solidifying her legacy as a visionary artist.

Lana Del Rey's influence spans beyond music, resonating deeply in fashion, art, and popular culture worldwide. Her devoted fanbase celebrates her unique style and emotive storytelling, contributing significantly to her enduring cultural impact. Throughout her career, Del Rey has continuously evolved artistically, experimenting with diverse sounds and themes while preserving her distinctive aesthetic. Her fearless exploration of new musical realms and artistic boundaries has established her as a pioneering figure in contemporary music.
